TQIP Rules — Quick Synopsis

PECs, RAVs, PMEs, and more (June 23, 2026). Tap a section to expand. Legend: ! = writing the exact PEC name alone gets no credit (extra detail required) · * = must list the medication.
PEC quick stats
  • PEC31 — 31 total PECs; goal is number above national average.
  • PEC8 — PECs associated with increased mortality: #3 Anticoagulation therapy, #8 Prior CVA, #9 COPD, #10 Chronic Renal Failure, #11 Cirrhosis, #12 CHF, #15 Dementia, #21 MI.
  • PEC3 — % of patients with 3 or more PECs; TQIP Table 12, column 6. National average is 34%.
Adult PECs (31)
  • ! Advanced Directive Limiting Care — no longer need a copy of the directive, but must reference that the pt has an Advanced Directive and what is "limited," e.g. "Advanced Directive Limiting Care — declining medications to support BP, or nutrition (or intubation, transfusion, surgery, etc.) — prior to admission." (NTDS Dictionary p72)
  • Alcohol Use Disorder — must use the words "Alcohol Use Disorder" (DSM-5).
    • Not acceptable: "Drinks," "alcoholic," "high BAC," etc.
  • * Anticoagulation therapy — need "Anticoagulation therapy on (LIST MED)." MUST list the medication (clopidogrel/Plavix count as anticoagulants for this PEC).
    • Not acceptable: just listing the med, or just "A/C Rx."
  • * Attention Deficit Disorder / ADHD — "Attention Deficit Hyperactive Disorder (list med)" — need the medication.
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder — "Autism Spectrum Disorder" or "Autism."
  • Bipolar — "Bipolar" is acceptable; does not need to be on current meds.
  • ! Bleeding disorder — state the disorder: "Bleeding Disorder — Thrombocytopenia," "Bleeding disorder — hemophilia," "von Willebrand's," "Factor V Leiden," or "Bleeding disorder — elevated PFA" (ASH 2015).
    • Not acceptable: just listing the issue.
  • ! Prior CVA — write "Prior CVA/stroke with cognitive dysfunction" or "Prior CVA/stroke with motor (or sensory) dysfunction."
    • Not acceptable: "Stroke," "CVA," "Uses walker," or "Prior stroke/CVA" without the deficit (commonly cognitive/impaired memory).
  • COPD — just list "COPD" (WHO 2019).
    • Not acceptable: interstitial fibrosis; "wife states he has COPD" (the trauma surgeon has to take responsibility).
  • ! Chronic Renal Failure — state "Chronic Renal Failure with Dialysis" or "Chronic Renal Failure that requires dialysis, but pt not going" (NTDS Data Dictionary p93).
  • Cirrhosis — "Cirrhosis" is acceptable; can be based on CHILD, MELD, or APRI <0.6, AST>2xALT or C/RL ratio >0.6 scores, imaging, laparotomy, or laparoscopy — all acceptable.
    • Not acceptable: "Abnormal LFTs"; attributing to another person ("Father states…") — take ownership.
  • CHF — "CHF" or "Chronic diastolic (or systolic) CHF with elevated BNaP" or "Chronic diastolic CHF treated with diuretic" (can use "acute" or "acute on chronic," and diastolic or systolic).
    • Not acceptable: "heart failure"; "spouse says pt has CHF" (need ownership).
    • Common manifestations: JVD, cardiomegaly, pulmonary engorgement (NTDS p98).
  • ! Current smoker — acceptable: "Current smoker within last 30 days."
    • Not acceptable: "Current smoker" (yes, you read that correctly — stating "Current smoker" will not get credit for the PEC "Current smoker").
  • * Chemotherapy — acceptable: "Chemotherapy for cancer (list med)."
  • Dementia — acceptable: "Dementia" or "Parkinson's Dementia" (National Institute on Aging 2017).
    • Not acceptable: "Daughter states he has dementia" (TS must take responsibility); "Parkinson's" alone (need the word Dementia).
  • * Diabetes mellitus — "Diabetes Mellitus on (LIST MED)" — need to list the medication!!
  • Disseminated cancer — "Disseminated cancer" or "Disseminated cancer with mets."
  • ! Functional dependent health status — "Functional dependent health status due to cognitive limitations" or "…due to physical limitations."
    • Not acceptable: "Functional Dependent Health Status" alone, "Pt has a walker," "Pt has cognitive issues."
  • * Hypertension — "Hypertension (LIST MED)" — need to list the medication.
  • Major Depressive Disorder — acceptable: "Major Depressive Disorder."
    • Not acceptable: "depression," "depression on medication," "mood disorder."
    • "Major" means the depression lasts 2 weeks or more, so anyone on meds for depression meets the criteria.
  • ! Myocardial infarction — acceptable: "Myocardial infarction within last 6 months" or "…within last 6 months, elevated troponin." If on admission, also add to PME (see below).
    • Not acceptable: "Myocardial infarction" alone — needs to be within 6 months.
  • Severe Obesity — BMI ≥ 40. Must be documented <24h (2026 ACS TQIP Benchmark Report References).
  • Other Mental Personality Disorders — the following 12 are acceptable:
    • Antisocial personality disorder
    • Avoidant personality disorder
    • Borderline personality disorder
    • Dependent personality disorder
    • Generalized Anxiety Disorder
    • Histrionic
    • Narcissistic personality disorder
    • Obsessive Compulsive Disorder
    • Obsessive compulsive personality disorder
    • Panic disorder
    • Paranoid
    • Schizotypal
    • NOT ACCEPTABLE: "anxiety," "Mood disorder," "personality do," "Dissociative," etc.
  • Peripheral arterial (or vascular) Disease — acceptable: "Peripheral arterial (or vascular) Disease" (CDC 2014; the 2024 fact sheet lists prevalence as 25-30% if >80 yo).
    • Not acceptable: "Husband states PAD," "atherosclerosis," "plaques."
  • Post Trauma Stress Disorder — acceptable: "Post Trauma Stress Disorder."
    • Not acceptable: "Stress disorder."
  • Pregnant — acceptable: "Pregnant."
    • Not acceptable: "Pt states she is pregnant," "elevated hCG."
  • Schizoaffective disorder — acceptable: "Schizoaffective Disorder."
  • Schizophrenia — acceptable: "Schizophrenia."
  • ! Steroid use (within 30 days for a chronic medical condition) — acceptable: "Steroids within 30 days for COPD" / "…for fibromyalgia" / "…for arthritis."
    • Not acceptable: "Steroids for arthritis" (no time stated), "steroid use."
  • * Substance Use Disorder — acceptable: "Substance Use Disorder — (substance used)," e.g. marijuana, cocaine, opioids, sedatives, hypnotics, benzos, etc. (DSM-5). NOTE: this does not require "abuse," just "use" — a pt getting controlled substances from their physician (anxiolytics, opioids, sedatives, hypnotics) for valid medical reasons still has substance use disorder.
    • Not acceptable: "uses opioids," "smokes marijuana," "abuses drugs," "sniffs glue" (MUST USE THE PHRASE "SUD — substance used").
  • Ventilator Dependence — must arrive with a tracheostomy.
! = using the exact words of the PEC will not get credit for these 8 · * = requires medication name for these 7.
7 PECs that need an associated medication
  • Anticoagulation therapy — list med
  • ADHD — list med
  • Chemotherapy — list med
  • Diabetes Mellitus — list med
  • Hypertension — list med
  • Steroid use — list med ("within 30 days" and for a stated chronic disease: COPD, arthritis, etc.)
  • Substance use disorder — need to list the substance
8 PECs where the exact phrase gets no credit
  • Advanced Directive Limiting Care — need to list what was limited (e.g. surgery, medications, etc.)
  • Bleeding disorder — need the disorder, e.g. thrombocytopenia
  • Prior stroke — need the deficit, e.g. "cognitive (or motor or sensory) dysfunction"
  • Chronic renal failure — "with HD" or "requires HD but pt not going"
  • Current smoker — within 30 days
  • Functional dependent health status — due to cognitive (or physical) limitations
  • Myocardial infarction — within 6 months
  • Steroid use — within 30 days for (list chronic disease, e.g. COPD, arthritis, etc.)
Risk Adjusted Variables (RAVs — 6)
  • Midline shift within 24h
  • Pupillary response abnormal (NPi < 3.0, or NPi difference > 0.5, pupil size > 4mm in a light environment, pupil difference > 3.0mm, or either pupil non-reactive)
  • Transfer from Outside Hospital
  • Pre-hospital cardiac arrest
  • Hypothermia (<35°C)
  • Fever (≥38°C)
Primary Medical Events (PMEs — 5)
Events that immediately preceded the traumatic injury.
  • Myocardial infarction POA — "Myocardial infarct with elevated troponins POA"
  • Sepsis POA
  • Cardiac arrest (not trauma related) POA
  • Intracranial bleeding not related to trauma (e.g. SAH from aneurysm) POA
  • Stroke (acute) POA
Injury patterns commonly not recognized
  • Pulmonary contusions — often labeled as atelectasis on CT; you can get credit if you add "images reviewed by trauma surgeon."
  • Laceration associated with >20% blood loss is a grade 3. This is typically a drop of Hb by >20% (e.g. 13.8 to 11 without transfusion; if transfusion is done, blood loss was likely over 20%). To get credit you MUST attribute the blood loss to the specific injury (or injuries, if appropriate).
  • Injuries that get an upgrade if associated with >20% blood loss:
    • Any named vessel in chest, abdomen, pelvis, or extremities
    • Pan-facial or LeFort 3
    • Hemothorax
    • Omentum
    • Pelvic ring
    • Penetrating injuries to face, neck, chest, abdomen, or extremities
  • Concussion with LOC — <0-59 min is grade 2; ≥1h is grade 3 — need to list the time.
  • DAI with LOC — 6-24h is grade 4; >24h is grade 5 (AIS 2015 p16).
  • Compression fx of vertebral body >20% is a grade 3.
  • Basilar skull fracture is a grade 3 (must state that it is basilar skull). Base of skull bones (5): 1) ethmoid, including lamina papyracea; 2) sphenoid; 3) occipital and condyles; 4) frontal — orbital roof; 5) temporal — petrous, squamous, and mastoid portions.
  • AIS 6 injuries:
    • Brainstem penetrating injury or laceration
    • Bilateral internal carotid or vertebral artery laceration
    • Bilateral sigmoid or transverse sinus laceration
    • Avulsion, crush, massive destruction of thorax
    • Thoracic aortic tear not confined to mediastinum
    • Bilateral pulmonary artery or vein laceration
    • Heart multiple lacerations (GSW or SW through-and-through) or ventricular rupture
    • Inhalation with mucosal sloughing, necrosis
    • Hepatic avulsion
    • Massive destruction of 2+ adjacent vertebrae
    • C3 quadriplegia and above
    • 90%+ burn
    • Whole-body explosion
  • List all injuries — the method used to evaluate the injury pattern allows 3 injuries in any ISS body area, and the 3 can be in the same body area. TQIP uses SRR (Survival Risk Ratio), which correlates better with NISS (New Injury Severity Score) than with ISS.

  • Scalp laceration to grade 3

  • Penetrating to grade 3

Abdomen
  • Skin laceration to grade 3
  • Penetrating to grade 3

  • Iliac artery to grade 4
  • Superior Mesenteric Artery (SMA) or main branches to grade 4
  • Named arteries to grade 4

  • IVC to grade 4
  • Named veins to grade 4

  • Pelvic ring fracture to grade 5
  • Mesentery laceration to grade 3, and grade 4 if "massive," "avulsion," or "complex"

Upper extremity
  • Penetrating above elbow to grade 3
  • Laceration to grade 3
  • Axillary artery to grade 3
  • Axillary vein to grade 3
  • Brachial artery to grade 3
  • Brachial vein to grade 3
  • Named arteries or veins to grade 3
Lower extremity
  • Laceration to grade 3
  • Penetrating at or above knee to grade 3
  • Femoral artery to grade 4
  • Femoral vein to grade 3
  • Popliteal artery to grade 3
  • Popliteal vein to grade 3
  • Named artery or vein to grade 3
Fractures that get upgraded (also listed above)
  • Pan-facial: from grade 3 to 4
  • LeFort 3: from grade 3 to 4
  • Pelvic ring with posterior arch involvement: from grade 3 or 4 to grade 5

Pediatric PECs (20)
Items 1-3 are Peds only (age 0-14). The rest follow the same documentation rules as the adult PECs above.
  • Broncho-pulmonary dysplasia / chronic lung disease (Peds age 0-14 only), e.g. cystic fibrosis.
  • Congenital anomalies (Peds age 0-14 only) — acceptable: "Congenital anomaly — spina bifida," "Congenital Anomaly — Down Syndrome," "Congenital anomaly — VSD."
    • Not acceptable: "Father states pt has tricuspid valve atresia" — take ownership, don't attribute to another person.
  • Prematurity (Peds age 0-14 only) — acceptable: "Prematurity" or born prior to 35 weeks.
  • ! Advanced Directive Limiting Care — reference the directive and what is "limited," e.g. "Advanced Directive Limiting Care — declining medications or aggressive care — prior to admission."
  • Alcohol Use Disorder — must use the words "Alcohol Use Disorder" (DSM-5). Not acceptable: "Drinks," "alcoholic," "high BAC," etc.
  • * Attention Deficit Disorder / ADHD — "Attention Deficit Hyperactive Disorder (list med)" — need the medication.
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder — "Autism Spectrum Disorder" or "Autism."
  • Bipolar — "Bipolar"; does not need to be on current meds.
  • ! Bleeding disorder — state the disorder: thrombocytopenia, hemophilia, von Willebrand's, Factor V Leiden, or elevated PFA. Not acceptable: just listing the issue.
  • COPD — just list "COPD." Not acceptable: interstitial fibrosis; "wife states he has COPD."
  • ! Current smoker — "Current smoker within last 30 days." Not acceptable: "Current smoker" alone.
  • * Diabetes mellitus — "Diabetes Mellitus on (LIST MED)" — need to list the medication!!
  • * Hypertension — "Hypertension (LIST MED)" — need to list the medication.
  • Major Depressive Disorder — "Major Depressive Disorder." Not acceptable: "depression," "depression on medication," "mood disorder." Anyone on meds for depression meets the criteria (major implies >2 weeks).
  • ! Myocardial infarction — needs to be within 6 months.
  • Severe Obesity — BMI ≥ 40 (2026 ACS TQIP Benchmark Report References).
  • Other Mental Personality Disorders — same 12 acceptable disorders as the adult list. NOT ACCEPTABLE: "anxiety," "Mood disorder," "personality do," "Dissociative," etc.
  • Post Trauma Stress Disorder — "Post Trauma Stress Disorder." Not acceptable: "Stress disorder."
  • Schizoaffective disorder — "Schizoaffective Disorder." / Schizophrenia — "Schizophrenia."
  • * Substance Use Disorder — "Substance Use Disorder — (substance used)." Does not require "abuse," just "use." Not acceptable: "uses opioids," "smokes marijuana," "abuses drugs," "sniffs glue."
